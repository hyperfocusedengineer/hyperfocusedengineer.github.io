Three steps to your first workout

HyperFit is iOS-only during the beta. You'll need an iPhone running iOS 16+ and the free Apple TestFlight app. The whole flow takes under two minutes.

01
Install TestFlight
Grab the free TestFlight app from Apple's App Store on your iPhone if you don't already have it.
02
Tap the invite link
Open the TestFlight invite on the same iPhone — it auto-opens TestFlight and surfaces an Accept button for HyperFit.
03
Install & lift
Tap Install, sign in with email or Google, log your first set. Updates push automatically as new builds ship.

Hit a bug? Shake your phone in TestFlight to send feedback — or email us directly. Beta is capped at 10,000 testers; if the link is full, open a GitHub issue and we'll add a slot.

What's in the build

Strength tracking, designed like Strava and Robinhood had a kid.

Anthracite base, strava-orange for the active set, robinhood-green when your session volume beats the last one. Mono numerics with tabular figures so every readout lines up. Every screen is built around one question: did the work happen, yes or no?

— 01
Anatomy-aware muscle heatmap
Recruitment-weighted volume per muscle, rendered onto a front/back silhouette. Toggle between volume mode and a recovery-decay model that shows what's actually fresh today.
— 02
Plan creator + active-plan rail
Build a multi-day split, assign sessions to days of the week, and the app schedules from there. One plan active at a time — no clutter.
— 03
Shareable workout cards
Every finished session generates a 1080×1350 card with your silhouette, top muscles, and PR badges — ready for stories. Re-share past sessions from history too.
— 04
PR detection
Per-exercise weight PRs detected the moment you log them. The 🔥 chip on the post-session screen and share card means it actually happened.
— 05
Ghost-value last-session inputs
Set fields pre-fill with what you lifted last time as faded placeholder text. No tap-to-clone — just a target you can beat or match without re-typing.
— 06
Real session history
Calendar deep-links into a session detail view with grouped sets per exercise, duration, total volume, and a share button that works months after the lift.
